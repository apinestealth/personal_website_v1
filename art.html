home iii. path home/home path
2021-present
materials: processing, projection
inspired by the concept of psychogeography, which reframes space through the lens of subjective experience, this instillation explores the themes of memory, home, and movement through/across a geographic place. this project combines the personal spatial experiences of my father and filters it through the lens of technology to reveal the fragmented ways in which we experience the places we call home. I am motivated by my horror at the ways in which settler colonialism, inequality, and a built environment centered around individual homes and the use of cars have disconnected us from community in the United States. by interacting with the physical projection of these translations, the viewer is invited into an intimate experience of a space that moves in and out of the digital and physical.
home ii. home boquet
2021
materials: 3d printer, paint
a meditation on the replicability of home & memorabilia of home using 3d models of places my family has lived for > 1 year. each home is arranged in the shape of an upside down and hanging bouquet, which is displayed inside my current home.

house portrait
2020
materials: watercolor, photography
my co-op and home on stanford's campus has a tradition of taking portaits of each resident and hanging them on the walls of our house. this project complicates the idea of a house portrait or series of portraits by animating digital portraits of each resident.
